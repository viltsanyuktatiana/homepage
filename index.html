Résumé

Monica Anna Maria Bellucci (born 30 September 1964) is an Italian actress and model. She began her career as a fashion model, modelling for Dolce & Gabbana and Dior, before making a transition to Italian films and later American films and French films.

Main information

She was born on September 30, 1964 in the city of Citta di Castello in the family of Pascual Bellucci and Brunella Brigantti, lived in the village of Lama (Lama), near San Justino, until she moved to France. She worked as a model to be able to pay for law school at the University of Perugia, but later left her studies and devoted herself to a modeling career.

In 1990, she debuted in the series Adult Children (directed by Dino Rizzi). And the following year she got the role of the main character in Francesco Laudadio's film Abuse (La riffa).

In 1992, she first played the role of one of Count Dracula's three maids in Bram Stoker's English-language film Dracula. Then he returned to Italy to shoot in the comedy Snowball (I mitici - Colpo gobbo a Milano). But her popularity spread in 2000 after the release of the film Malèna (Malèna). Thus, Monica Bellucci received the status of "Virgin" and became known and appreciated first in Europe and then in America.

In 2003 she was a guest of honor at the Cannes Film Festival. He plays Persephone in The Matrix: Reboot and The Matrix: Revolution, and then Mary Magdalene in Mel Gibson's The Passion of the Christ. After gaining experience abroad, he returned to Italy in 2003 and landed one of the lead roles in Gabriel Muccino's Ricordati di me. He was nominated for the David di Donatello National Italian Film Award and won a silver ribbon.

In 2005 she played a witch in Terry Gilliam's film The Brothers Grimm and Daniela, an Italian prostitute in How Much Do You Cost ?. In 2006, Paolo Verzi offered her the role of a happy and pretty baroness in the film N (Io e Napoleone). This film opens the first Rome International Film Festival.

In 2008 she took part in the Cannes Film Festival with an Italian film outside the competition of Marco Tullio Jordan's Mad Blood (Sanguepazzo), where she played the famous Italian actress of the Second World War Louise Ferrido. In the same year he opened the third Rome International Film Festival, with the film L'uomo che ama.

In March 2009, she was awarded the World Actress Award at the Women's World Awards. At the Berlin Film Festival in 2009, she presented The Private Lives of Pippa Lee (a film by Rebecca Miller), where she starred with other famous actors, such as Winona Ryder, Keanu Reeves and others. On September 27, 2009, she was one of those who signed an appeal for the release of director Roman Polyansky, who was detained in Switzerland pending extradition to the United States.

In 2015, Monica starred in the 24th film "007 Spectrum" from the series "Bondiana", where she played Lucia Skiarra, the girlfriend of James Bond.
